The **preference center** is a public page (no login required) where a subscriber manages what they receive from you. They reach it through a secure, tokenized link in the footer of every email.

This is the subscriber-facing side of your [topics](/en/guide/topics) and consent, and it's what keeps you compliant: every email must link to it.

## What a subscriber can do

**Manage topics**

A subscriber sees each topic with its current state (*Consent granted*, *Pending confirmation*, *Consent revoked* or *No consent*) and can **grant** or **revoke** consent per topic individually.

**Pause emails**

They can temporarily **pause all emails** for a chosen number of days without changing any consents. A banner shows *Paused until {date}*, and they can **Resume now** at any time.

**Revoke all**

They can revoke consent for **all** topics and stop receiving any email, with a confirmation step. When someone arrives via an unsubscribe link, the page prompts them to confirm; after revoking, they land on a confirmation page.

## Expired links

Preference-center links are time-limited. If a link has expired, the subscriber sees a notice explaining they can find a fresh link in the footer of any recent email.

<Note>
  You don't build this page; Railmail hosts it. Configure the footer wording and links under **Settings → Email**. See [Sending domain](/en/guide/sending-domain#email-footer).
</Note>
